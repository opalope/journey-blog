My Web Dev Journey

December 27th, 2021

Tonight is an unusual snowy night in the Pacific Northwest, and I'm here, learning, and practicing the foundations and essentials to software development, primarily web development.

I officially started my endeavor in late August, 2021, learning the basics of HTML and CSS through freecodecamp.org. With the worries of a fully independent twenty-year-old, I had shifted my focus towards aspects of my life that needed my attention. The code would have to hold off for a while.

Over the course of a couple months, as my patience with working dead-end jobs was growing thin, I started seriously debating what I wanted to do as a career. I thought back to August, and started researching my options of becoming a software developer. I started back up in November, 2021. I have been using The Odin Project as a curriculum to teach myself the foundations to create efficient, functional, and aesthetically pleasing websites.

I still have a lot to learn, but that's why I am creating this blog; to document the beginning of what Iâ€™m hoping to be a long, fruitful journey.

January 2, 2022

Happy New Year! I'm two days into the new year and I have set some pretty exciting goals for myself! My biggest goal that I will achieve is to land a Junior Software Developing position. I would love for 2022 to be the year I start my career. With everything going on in the past two years, such as Covid-19, political division, exorbitant rental prices, etc., the start of my twenties has been nothing but uncertain.

Since my last entry, I have learned more about CSS, such as the Box Model, Block and Inline, Flexbox and its properties (growing/shrinking, main axis/cross axis, alignment). These concepts have been relatively easy to learn, but applying is a little more complicated. I'm enjoying the process of it all.

In the Odin Project, I have been doing various exercises they have created in Visual Studio Code. What I enjoy the most is they don't walk you through it, I have to figure it out. There have been moments where the code wasn't doing what I wanted it to :P, so I would step away for a couple minutes, let my brain deflate, then come back. Majority of the time, the solution pops up in my head. My next assignment will be to create a more professional looking website, so I'm excited. I'll write about how it goes :).
