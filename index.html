The Dev of the Web

The Tales of My Journey in Software Developing

December 27th, 2021

Tonight is an unusual snowy night in the Pacific Northwest, and I'm here, learning, and practicing the foundations and essentials to software development, primarily web development.

I officially started my endeavor in late August, 2021, learning the basics of HTML and CSS through freecodecamp.org. With the worries of a fully independent twenty-year-old, I had shifted my focus towards aspects of my life that needed my attention. The code would have to hold off for a while.

Over the course of a couple months, as my patience with working dead-end jobs was growing thin, I started seriously debating what I wanted to do as a career. I thought back to August, and started researching my options of becoming a software developer. I started back up in November, 2021. I have been using The Odin Project as a curriculum to teach myself the foundations to create efficient, functional, and aesthetically pleasing websites.

I still have a lot to learn, but that’s why I am creating this blog; to document the beginning of what I’m hoping to be a long, fruitful journey.
